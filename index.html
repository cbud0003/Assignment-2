OLYMPICS

1896 - 2012

The Olympic Games are an international sporting event, held every four years. The ultimate aims are to nurture human beings and contribute to world peace via sport. Summer and winter games are held at separate times. (About The Olympic Games, n.d.) The first Olympic Games was first held in Athens, Greece in 1986, where athletes consist of all males (Athens 1896 Olympic Games, n.d.). Female competed for the first time at the 1900 Games that was held in Paris (When Did Women First Compete in the Olympic Games, 2021). In research from The Guardian (2017), more than 35,000 medals have been awarded at the Olympics since 1896. This visualization aims to explore more about which athletes have won the most medals and which country do they came from and how has it changed over time.

Medals won across the World (1984 - 2012)

At first sight, the darker region surrounding the United States indicates that it has won the most medals over the years - a total of 4,585 medals. Following the next most noticeable darker regions are, Australia, Russia and China, with a total wins of 1,189 , 768, and 807 medals, respectively. Some notable mentions of countries with many medals won include, Canada (649), France (1,395), and Sweden (1,044).

Men vs Women

At first glance, the participants have largely been men over the years. The first ever female particpants recorded was in 1900 with a total of 11 participants, the number gradually increases over the years. Meanwhile, the male participants can be seen consistent over the years, with 1,255 participants as the highest ever recorded in 1920 and lowest was in 1896 - only 151 in total. As for the female, the highest recorded number of participants is 932 in 2008.
A trend that can be seen in this graph is that male participants are consistently higher than female participants, eventhough there has been an increase in female participants since 1900, the male participants are seemingly always higher and never once recorded lower than the female participants.

to view exact number of participants in each year, hover over the graph


Bronze vs Silver vs Gold

Naturally, as the number of participants increase, so does the number of medals. It started with only 151 medals in 1896 to a total of 1,949 medals in 2012. There are no particular trends in which medal type is awarded most as it keeps on changing every year. For example, in year 1928, Bronze is the highest medal type awarded but in 1932, the highest is Gold. However,the accumulated number of medals from year 1896 - 2012 is 31,165, with Gold as the leading medal type - a total number of 10,486 , followed by Bronze (10,369) and Silver (10,310).
